<threat_model>
STRIDE register:
- Information disclosure: Queue/scheduler topology, queue depth, task IDs, raw prompts, auth material, API keys, provider secrets, and routing internals must not appear in ordinary responses or high-cardinality labels. Disposition: low-cardinality metric allowlist and response assertions.
- Elevation of privilege: User-authored prompt content or untrusted request priority must not produce high priority beyond policy. Disposition: PriorityResolver trust hierarchy, max-priority policy, high quota, and downgrade tests.
- Denial of service: Queue or Scheduler failures must not stop forwarding, while hard caps still protect the process. Disposition: FIFO fallback, MemoryQueue fallback, QueueGuard hard cap, cancellation cleanup, and strict-mode-only rejection.
- Repudiation: Priority downgrades need operator visibility without sensitive payloads. Disposition: sanitized downgrade reason metrics/logs only.
</threat_model>
